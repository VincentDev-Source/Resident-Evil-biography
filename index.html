The History of Resident Evil

Resident Evil is a long-running survival horror saga built on secret research, biological weapons, corporate conspiracy, and repeated global outbreaks. From Resident Evil 0 to Resident Evil 8, the story follows how Umbrella, rival bioweapon groups, cults, and hidden labs turned scientific ambition into a chain of disasters stretching from the Arklay Mountains to rural Europe.

Threat Overview

The tone of Resident Evil changed over time, but the core pattern stayed the same: secret research creates unstable power, corporations and cults try to control it, and ordinary people are left inside the outbreak. Every major game adds another layer of betrayal, mutation, and survival.

Lore Timeline

The games connect through escalating biohazards. Each major entry expands the consequences of illegal experimentation, military misuse, black-market sales, and the survivors forced to stop it.

Before the outbreaks: Progenitor, Umbrella, and the birth of B.O.W. research

Long before the mansion incident, Oswell E. Spencer, Edward Ashford, and James Marcus built the foundations of Umbrella around the Progenitor virus. Their research eventually produced the T-virus, creating the blueprint for zombies, Tyrants, and the earliest bio-organic weapons.

Resident Evil 0: Rebecca Chambers, Billy Coen, and the Ecliptic Express

Resident Evil 0 begins with S.T.A.R.S. medic Rebecca Chambers investigating murders in the Arklay Mountains. She crosses paths with escaped prisoner Billy Coen and uncovers the ruined training school run by James Marcus. The game explains how Marcus’s revenge and Umbrella’s infected leeches helped trigger the larger disaster around Raccoon City.

Resident Evil: Chris, Jill, Wesker, and the Spencer Mansion

Chris Redfield, Jill Valentine, Barry Burton, Albert Wesker, and the rest of S.T.A.R.S. Alpha Team enter the Spencer Mansion while investigating the disappearance of Bravo Team. Inside they discover Umbrella labs, B.O.W.s, and Wesker’s betrayal. The mansion incident is the moment the central heroes learn that Umbrella is behind the horror.

Resident Evil 2: Leon, Claire, Ada, Sherry, and the G-virus

In the ruins of Raccoon City, rookie police officer Leon S. Kennedy and college student Claire Redfield try to survive the outbreak. They meet Ada Wong, Sherry Birkin, and the mutated William Birkin, whose G-virus research turns the city into something even worse than a T-virus zombie outbreak.

Resident Evil 3: Jill Valentine, Carlos Oliveira, and Nemesis

Jill tries to escape collapsing Raccoon City while being hunted by Nemesis, a Tyrant deployed to kill S.T.A.R.S. members. Carlos Oliveira and the U.B.C.S. become key allies as the city falls beyond saving. By the end of the crisis, the U.S. government destroys Raccoon City to contain the infection.

Resident Evil 4: Leon, Ashley, Ada, and Las Plagas

Years after Raccoon City, Leon works as a government agent and is sent to Spain to rescue Ashley Graham, the U.S. president’s daughter. There he faces Osmund Saddler, Ramon Salazar, Bitores Mendez, and the parasite cult Los Illuminados. Ada returns, proving that biohazard trafficking continues even after Umbrella’s collapse.

Resident Evil 5: Chris, Sheva, Wesker, and Uroboros

Chris Redfield and Sheva Alomar investigate a bioweapon deal in Kijuju, Africa and uncover a wider conspiracy involving Tricell, Excella Gionne, Ricardo Irving, and Albert Wesker. The story brings the long Umbrella era to a climax as Wesker attempts to reshape humanity through Uroboros.

Resident Evil 6: Leon, Chris, Jake, Ada, and a global C-virus war

Resident Evil 6 interweaves several campaigns. Leon and Helena Harper face a national catastrophe, Chris and Piers Nivans battle international bioterror, Jake Muller and Sherry Birkin become central to the search for a cure, and Ada Wong becomes tangled in a plot involving Derek C. Simmons and Carla Radames. This is the series at its largest scale.

Resident Evil 7: Ethan, Mia, the Bakers, and Eveline

The series pivots back to intimate horror when Ethan Winters searches for Mia at the Baker estate in Louisiana. Jack, Marguerite, Lucas, and Eveline reveal a new threat built around the Mold rather than classic viruses or parasites. Chris Redfield reappears at the end, linking the new story to the older anti-biohazard world.

Resident Evil Village: Ethan, Rose, Chris, and the Four Lords

Resident Evil 8 follows Ethan into a remote village ruled by Mother Miranda and her Four Lords: Lady Dimitrescu, Donna Beneviento, Salvatore Moreau, and Karl Heisenberg. The game reveals how Miranda, the Megamycete, and the Mold connect to Ethan’s family, Mia, Rose Winters, and Chris Redfield’s mission to stop another catastrophic mutation.

Key Lore Pillars

Umbrella Corporation

The central architect of the early disasters. Even after its collapse, Umbrella’s research survives through stolen samples, rival corporations, and state actors.

B.O.W. Evolution

Zombies were only the beginning. Hunters, Tyrants, parasites, Mold creatures, and enhanced soldiers all show the series’ constant escalation in biological horror.

Recurring Survivors

Characters like Leon, Chris, Jill, Ada, Claire, Rebecca, Sherry, Carlos, Sheva, Jake, and Ethan represent different sides of the conflict: soldiers, spies, civilians, medics, and unwilling victims pulled into history-changing events.

Wesker’s Shadow

Albert Wesker connects multiple eras of the franchise. Even after his downfall, the effects of his research, plans, allies, and bloodline continue through later conflicts such as Jake Muller’s story.

The Winters Era

Ethan, Mia, and Rose Winters pull the series into a more personal type of terror while still proving that every new monster comes from people trying to control life itself.

Major Characters Across Resident Evil 0 to 8

The six profile pages in the navbar cover your featured characters, but the wider lore involves a much larger cast. These figures shape the series from the mansion era to Village.

Resident Evil 0 to 1 Era

Rebecca Chambers, Billy Coen, Chris Redfield, Jill Valentine, Barry Burton, and Albert Wesker define the original crisis around the Ecliptic Express, the training facility, and the Spencer Mansion.

Raccoon City Era

Leon S. Kennedy, Claire Redfield, Ada Wong, Sherry Birkin, Marvin Branagh, Carlos Oliveira, Nicholai Ginovaef, and William Birkin become central to the fall of Raccoon City and the spread of the G-virus.

Post-Umbrella Era

Leon, Ashley Graham, Luis Serra, Ada Wong, Sheva Alomar, Josh Stone, Ingrid Hunnigan, and merchant networks define the series’ shift from one corporation’s crimes to worldwide bio-terror.

Resident Evil 5 to 6 Era

Chris Redfield, Sheva Alomar, Piers Nivans, Helena Harper, Jake Muller, Sherry Birkin, Ada Wong, Derek C. Simmons, Carla Radames, and Albert Wesker dominate the global escalation period.

Resident Evil 7 Era

Ethan Winters, Mia Winters, Zoe Baker, Jack Baker, Marguerite Baker, Lucas Baker, Eveline, and the Blue Umbrella-connected Chris Redfield shape the Mold storyline.

Resident Evil 8 Era

Ethan Winters, Mia Winters, Rose Winters, Chris Redfield, Mother Miranda, Lady Dimitrescu, Donna Beneviento, Salvatore Moreau, Karl Heisenberg, and the Duke drive the final phase of Ethan’s story.
